Situational Leadership Overview

Hersey & Blanchard · Adaptive Leadership
▼

The Situational Leadership Model was developed by Paul Hersey and Ken Blanchard. Its core idea is simple: there is no single best leadership style. Instead, a great leader adapts their approach depending on the person they are leading and the specific task at hand.

A leader looks at two things about a team member:

  • Competence - Does this person have the skills and knowledge to do the task?
  • Commitment - Is this person motivated and confident enough to do it?

Based on these two factors, the leader selects one of four styles ranging from Directing (high control) to Delegating (high freedom).

💡 Analogy
Think of a driving instructor. With a complete beginner, they grip the wheel and give constant instructions (Directing). As the student improves, they coach, then support, and finally just sit back and let the driver handle everything (Delegating). The instructor changed style - the car did not.
▶ Real-World Example
A new employee joins a tech company. The manager does not hand them a project and disappear. The manager shows them step-by-step how things work, closely checks their work, and slowly gives more independence as skills grow. This is Situational Leadership in action.
✦ Summary
Situational Leadership = Adapt your style to the person and the task. Two key variables: competence + commitment. Styles: Directing, Coaching, Supporting, Delegating.

نموذج Situational Leadership اتطور على إيد Paul Hersey وKen Blanchard. الفكرة الأساسية: مفيش أسلوب قيادة واحد بيصلح في كل الحالات. القائد الكويس هو اللي بيغير أسلوبه حسب الشخص اللي قدامه والمهمة المطلوبة.

القائد بيحتاج يحكم على الموظف من ناحيتين:

  • Competence ، عنده المهارة والمعرفة عشان يعمل الشغل؟
  • Commitment ، مؤمن بنفسه وعنده دافع عشان يكمل؟
💡 تشبيه
فكر في مدرب قيادة سيارة. مع المبتدئ بيمسك العجلة ويعطي تعليمات مستمرة (Directing). لما الطالب يتحسن بيتدرج للفراغ الكامل (Delegating). الأسلوب اتغير والسيارة ما اتغيرتش.
✦ ملخص
Situational Leadership = غير أسلوبك حسب الشخص والمهمة. متغيرين أساسيين: competence + commitment.

Matching Development Levels to Leadership Styles
▼

The model maps four Development Levels (D1-D4) to four Leadership Styles (S1-S4). Each level combines competence and commitment.

D2SomeLowS2 CoachingHas some skills but feels discouraged or unsure.

D4HighHighS4 DelegatingExpert, self-motivated. Can be fully trusted.

⚠ Common Mistake
Leaders often over-use one style. A manager who always Directs will frustrate skilled employees. A manager who always Delegates will leave new staff confused. Also remember: a D4 on one task may be D1 on a completely new task.
▶ Example
Ahmed (D1): Just joined sales, super excited but never sold before. Manager uses S1 Directing - clear daily tasks, close supervision.

Sara (D4): 8 years of experience, consistently hits targets. Manager uses S4 Delegating - assigns the goal and steps back.
✦ Summary
Match D1 to S1, D2 to S2, D3 to S3, D4 to S4. Development level is task-specific - the same person can be at different levels for different tasks.

The Process: Using the Right Style

3-Step Framework to Apply Situational Leadership
▼

Applying Situational Leadership is a practical 3-step process.

  1. Step 1 - Identify the Task: Be specific. What exactly needs to be done? What skills does it require? Vague tasks lead to vague assessments.
  2. Step 2 - Assess Readiness: Evaluate the individual's competence and commitment for that specific task. Is this person D1, D2, D3, or D4 for this task? This changes per task, not per person overall.
  3. Step 3 - Adapt the Style: Apply the matching style. D1 = Directing, D2 = Coaching, D3 = Supporting, D4 = Delegating.
💡 Analogy
A doctor does not give all patients the same medicine. First they diagnose (Identify + Assess), then prescribe (Adapt Style). A leader does the same.
▶ Worked Example
Task: Present to the board of directors.
Employee: Khalid - highly experienced in finance (D4 for financial reports) but has never presented to a board before (D1 for board presentations).
Manager correctly identifies this as D1 and uses Directing/Coaching, not Delegating.
✦ Summary
Steps: (1) Define the task, (2) Assess the D-level for that task, (3) Match the S-style. Skipping any step leads to mismatched leadership and poor outcomes.

تطبيق Situational Leadership عملية من 3 خطوات.

  1. الخطوة 1 - تحديد المهمة: كن محددا. إيه بالظبط المطلوب? إيه المهارات المطلوبة?
  2. الخطوة 2 - تقييم الجاهزية: قيّم competence و commitment للموظف في المهمة المحددة دي.
  3. الخطوة 3 - تكييف الأسلوب: طبّق الأسلوب المناسب.
💡 تشبيه
الدكتور مش بيدي كل المرضى نفس الدوا. أولا بيشخّص (تحديد + تقييم)، بعدين بيوصّف العلاج (تكييف الأسلوب). القائد بيعمل نفس الشيء.
✦ ملخص
الخطوات: (1) حدّد المهمة، (2) قيّم مستوى D، (3) طابق الأسلوب S. لو حذفت أي خطوة هتاخذ نتيجة غلط.

Goleman's 6 Leadership Styles

Emotional Intelligence-Based Leadership Framework
▼

Daniel Goleman introduced 6 leadership styles rooted in Emotional Intelligence (EI). Unlike Hersey and Blanchard, Goleman focused on the emotional climate a leader creates and how that affects team performance.

#StyleKey PhraseEI CompetenciesBest Used When
1CoerciveDo what I tell youSelf-control, initiativeCrisis or turnaround
2AuthoritativeCome with meSelf-confidence, empathyNew direction needed
3AffiliativePeople come firstEmpathy, communicationHeal conflict or boost morale
4DemocraticWhat do you think?Collaboration, communicationBuild consensus or buy-in
5PacesettingDo as I do, nowInitiative, conscientiousnessHigh-motivated skilled team
6CoachingTry thisEmpathy, self-awarenessDevelop people long-term
💡 Analogy
Think of Goleman's 6 styles like tools in a toolbox. A carpenter does not use only a hammer. The right tool depends on what you are building. A great leader knows all 6 tools and picks the right one based on the team's situation.
⚠ Important Note
Goleman found that 4 styles (Authoritative, Affiliative, Democratic, Coaching) have a positive impact on team climate. Coercive and Pacesetting can have negative effects if overused. They are powerful but dangerous.
✦ Summary
Goleman = 6 EI-based styles. Best leaders use multiple styles fluidly. Most positively impactful: Authoritative, Coaching, Affiliative, Democratic. Riskiest if overused: Coercive, Pacesetting.

Characteristics of the Six Styles

Deep Dive into Each Style's Behaviors
▼
▶ 1 - Pacesetting
Sets extremely high standards and expects others to match them. Obsessed with speed and quality. If someone falls short, steps in and does it themselves. Has little empathy for poor performance. Risk: burns out the team and creates anxiety.
▶ 2 - Commanding / Coercive
Gives direct orders, rarely asks for input. Controls through tight monitoring. Motivates through fear of consequences. Can dismiss other approaches as soft. Useful in genuine emergencies but damaging long-term.
▶ 3 - Visionary / Authoritative
Articulates a compelling future. Brings people along by explaining the why. Invites input but maintains final authority. Most broadly effective style - works in almost any situation.
▶ 4 - Affiliative
Cares deeply about the whole person, not just work output. Shares their own emotions with the team. Rewards both results AND how people treat each other. Risk: may avoid difficult performance conversations.
▶ 5 - Democratic
Trusts the team to guide decisions. Reaches conclusions by consensus. Holds lots of meetings, listens deeply. Rarely gives negative feedback. Risk: decision paralysis during a crisis.
▶ 6 - Coaching
Identifies each person's strengths and development areas. Provides continuous feedback. May sacrifice short-term performance for long-term development. Assigns stretch assignments. Fails when the person is not interested in growing.
✦ Summary
Pacesetting and Commanding: powerful but risky. Visionary: most versatile. Affiliative: heals but avoids hard truths. Democratic: empowers but can stall. Coaching: develops people but needs a willing learner.
▶ 1 - Pacesetting
بيحدّد معايير أداء عالية جدًا. لو حد قصّر بينزل يعمل الشغل بنفسه. خطر: بيحرق الفريق ويسبّب توتر.
▶ 2 - Commanding
بيأمر مباشرة، نادرًا يسأل عن رأي حد. بيتحكم بمراقبة مستمرة. مفيد في الأزمات بس ضار على المدى الطويل.
▶ 3 - Visionary
بيشرح الرؤية المستقبلية بشكل مقنع. الأكثر فعالية بشكل عام ويشتغل في معظم المواقف.
▶ 4 - Affiliative
بيهتم بالإنسان كله مش بس شغله. خطر: ممكن يتجنّب محادثات الأداء الصعبة.
▶ 5 - Democratic
بيثق في الفريق. بيوصّل للقرارات بالإجماع. خطر: في الأزمات ممكن يتأخّر في القرار.
▶ 6 - Coaching
بيحدّد نقاط قوة وضعف كل شخص. بيدي feedback مستمر. بيفشل لو الموظف مش عنده رغبة في التطور.

⚠ The Golden Rule
No style is universally right or wrong. Great leaders assess: (1) Task urgency, (2) Team skill and motivation, (3) Organizational culture, (4) Long-term vs short-term needs. Then they choose.
▶ Real Scenario
A company is losing money fast. The CEO uses Commanding to stop the bleeding. Once stabilized, switches to Visionary to rebuild direction. Then uses Democratic to build a new strategy with the leadership team. Finally uses Coaching to develop managers for the future. That is fluid leadership - 4 styles in one situation.
✦ Summary
Every style has a "when it works" and a "when it fails." The key is reading the room: urgency, skill level, motivation, and culture. The most versatile leaders switch styles like a conductor switches tempo.

Leveraging Strengths & Blind Spots

Personal SWOT Analysis for Leaders
▼

Self-awareness is the foundation of great leadership. A Personal SWOT Analysis is a self-assessment tool leaders use to evaluate themselves honestly and grow.

LetterMeaningLeadership Application
SStrengthsWhat leadership skills do I excel at? (communication, vision...)
WWeaknessesWhere do I struggle? (giving negative feedback, delegating...)
OOpportunitiesWhat external chances exist to grow? (training, mentors, new roles)
TThreatsWhat could derail my leadership? (blind spots, biases, overusing a style)

After your SWOT, reflect on three critical questions:

  1. Which strength do you rely on most? Overusing even a strength can become a weakness.
  2. Could overusing that strength cause challenges? A very Democratic leader may be unable to make fast decisions in a crisis.
  3. Which strengths are underdeveloped, and why? Maybe you have never tried Coaching because you fear vulnerability. That is a blind spot.
💡 Analogy
When driving, there is a zone your mirrors cannot see - the blind spot. In leadership, a blind spot is a style you never use or always overuse without realizing its impact. You must actively turn and look to see it.
▶ Example
A manager always uses Pacesetting because she is highly competent. She does not realize her team is stressed and afraid to make mistakes. Her strength (high standards) became her blind spot (killing psychological safety). A Personal SWOT would reveal this.
✦ Summary
Personal SWOT = Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats applied to yourself as a leader. Goal: be self-aware and intentional. Your biggest strength, when overused, often becomes your biggest threat.

الوعي الذاتي هو أساس القيادة الحقيقية. Personal SWOT Analysis أداة للقائد يقيّم فيها نفسه بصدق ويحدّد مجالات تطوره.

💡 تشبيه
لما بتقود عربية، فيه منطقة المرايا مش بتشوفها - الـ blind spot. في القيادة، الـ blind spot هو أسلوب بتستخدمه أو بتتجنّبه من غير ما تحس بتأثيره على الفريق.
✦ ملخص
Personal SWOT = تطبيق التحليل على نفسك كقائد. الهدف الوعي الذاتي والتعمّد. أقوى نقطة عندك لو بالغت فيها هتبقى أكبر تهديد عليك.
